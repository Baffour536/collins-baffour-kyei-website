ECO 107

Introduction to Computing for Social Scientists

A practical introduction to computer applications for economic and financial data management and visualisation. The course develops working competence in Microsoft Excel and R using real economic and financial examples, with particular attention to the Ghanaian context.

Excel data managementDescriptive statisticsData visualisationDashboardsR programmingData cleaningEconomic data
LevelFirst Year
Teaching roleCourse Instructor
Academic contextUndergraduate · Three hours per week
InstitutionDepartment of Economic Studies · School of Economics · University of Cape Coast
Learning focus

What students should be able to do.

  • Use Excel and R for data management and visualisation.
  • Manage economic and financial datasets in a structured and reproducible manner.
  • Analyse Ghana-relevant economic data and communicate data-driven insights.
  • Move from spreadsheet-based analysis into introductory R programming and data workflows.

Course roadmap

Topics and learning progression.

The roadmap below reflects the course outline.

Week 1

Introduction to Computing for Social Scientists

Role of computing in the social sciences; data types; overview of Excel, R, SPSS and Python.

Week 2

Excel for Economic Applications

Excel interface and navigation; SUM, AVERAGE, MIN and MAX; simple economic indicators.

Week 3

Data Entry and Management in Excel

Entering, editing and formatting data; sorting; filtering; data validation and error checking.

Week 4

Descriptive Statistics in Excel

Mean, median, mode, variance and standard deviation; Data Analysis ToolPak; interpretation.

Week 5

Data Visualisation in Excel

Bar, line, pie and scatter charts; formatting visualisations for clarity and economic communication.

Week 6

Visualisation and Data Analysis in Excel

Data Analysis Tools, Solver, dashboards, regression and introductory forecasting techniques.

Week 7

Introduction to R Programming

R and RStudio; commands, syntax and scripts; introductory arithmetic and computational thinking.

Week 8

Data Importation and Cleaning in R

Import CSV and Excel files; tidyverse-oriented cleaning; missing data and error detection.

Week 9

Descriptive Statistics in R

summary(), sd(), var() and dplyr-based summaries using economic datasets.

Week 10

Data Visualisation in R

ggplot2 and base plotting; bar charts, scatter plots and line graphs for economic trends.

Week 11

Advanced Topics and Practical Integration

Introductory lm(); PivotTables; VLOOKUP and INDEX-MATCH; integrated social science case studies.

Week 12

Recap and Assessment

Integrated review, practical Excel and R assessment, challenge discussion and course feedback.
