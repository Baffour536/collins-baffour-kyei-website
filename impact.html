Academic Impact & Leadership

Turning quantitative expertise into training, institutional programmes and community-facing data literacy.

My public-engagement and academic-service work increasingly focuses on making data, research methods and responsible technology use more accessible—from postgraduate classrooms to Senior High Schools, online learners, traders, artisans and national stakeholder platforms.

Flagship programme · 2026

UCC Data Literacy Week 2026

Contributed to the organising and implementation of a Bank of Ghana-sponsored multi-component initiative under the theme Building a Data-Smart Ghana: Evidence, Ethics and Inclusion.

400+Online learners in foundational Data Science and AI training
130+SHS students reached through mentorship
500+Stakeholders at the final conference

The programme connected stakeholder consultation, teacher and mentor preparation, online training, Senior High School mentorship, community outreach to artisans and traders, and a national stakeholder conference involving major public institutions.

Programme pathway

Six connected components, from institutional preparation to national dialogue.

Component 01

Stakeholder consultation

Supported the pathway for establishing Data Science Outreach and Mentorship Clubs in seven selected Senior High Schools through early faculty, teacher, mentor and postgraduate engagement.

Component 02

Teacher and mentor preparation

An Excel-first train-the-trainer model prepared teachers and mentors to facilitate beginner-friendly data literacy, club activities and applied analytical exercises.

Component 03

Online Data Science & AI training

A six-weekend, twelve-session online programme introduced more than 400 participants to Python, Google Colab, data analysis, visualisation, Generative AI and responsible digital practice.

Component 04

SHS mentorship

Twelve UCC mentors reached more than 130 students across seven schools with practical Excel, PivotTable, chart and dashboard training while establishing school-based mentorship clubs.

Component 05

Community outreach

Artisans and traders were engaged on record-keeping, sales and expense tracking, inventory monitoring, customer understanding and the practical value of data in small-business decisions.

Component 06

Stakeholders conference

A 500+ participant conference brought together academia, national institutions, students and community stakeholders, alongside a Datathon and the launch of the Dwumadie Tracker.

2026 international capacity building

Academic workshop coordination and implementation support.

Two intensive June 2026 programmes expanded the School of Economics' engagement with responsible AI, Bayesian reasoning and modern impact evaluation.

15–19 June 2026 · UCC Coordination Team

Artificial Intelligence in Higher Education

Served on the UCC Coordination Team for a five-day Professors Without Borders-supported workshop. Supported reception and institutional orientation of the international facilitator and programme activities on andragogy, experiential learning, TPACK, SAMR, AI ethics, cognitive debt, cognitive offloading, automation bias, Human in the Loop, Design Thinking and AI-supported entrepreneurship.

22–26 June 2026 · Organising support

Bayesian Networks and Impact Evaluation in Economics

Supported a five-day intensive quantitative training programme for faculty, researchers and graduate students. The programme combined conceptual sessions and R laboratories on Multinomial, Gaussian and Dynamic Bayesian Networks, the Kalman Filter, RCTs, Propensity Score Matching, Difference-in-Differences and Regression Discontinuity Design, culminating in the integration of causal impact estimates into Bayesian Network structures.

International academic capacity building

Artificial Intelligence in Higher Education.

In June 2026, I served on the UCC Coordination Team for an international capacity-building workshop on Artificial Intelligence in Higher Education, facilitated by Professor Jawad Khan of the University of Southern California, USA, with support from Professors Without Borders.

  • Supported the coordination and delivery of a multi-day academic engagement for lecturers, graduate assistants and students.
  • Engaged with pedagogical frameworks including TPACK and SAMR for the responsible integration of AI into teaching and course design.
  • Explored ethical issues surrounding integrity, trust, plagiarism, intellectual property, fairness, transparency and bias in AI-assisted education.
  • Examined cognitive challenges associated with AI use, including cognitive offloading, automation bias and the importance of Human-in-the-Loop decision-making.
  • Participated in applied sessions connecting Artificial Intelligence with entrepreneurship, Design Thinking, business idea generation and innovation.
  • Supported international academic engagement and institutional collaboration involving the School of Economics and Professors Without Borders.
Gender-responsive academic capacity building

Equipping Women for Impact in Teaching, Research and Leadership.

In December 2025, I supported the organisation and implementation of a two-day University of Cape Coast capacity-building programme for female academics under the NORHED-funded ECOGIV initiative. Delivered through the Centre for Data Archiving, Management, Analysis and Advocacy, the engagement formed part of a wider international partnership connecting UCC with Nha Trang University in Vietnam and IPB University in Indonesia.

  • Supported programme coordination and implementation alongside the CDAMAA and Department of Data Science and Economic Policy team.
  • Contributed to an academic capacity-building environment focused on research data management, responsible data practices and open science.
  • Supported engagement around digital teaching tools and the purposeful integration of technology into university teaching and learning.
  • Participated in discussions on publication planning, research development and strategies for strengthening the visibility and impact of academic work.
  • Engaged with issues of flexible work arrangements, workload management and institutional practices that can support women to thrive in academic careers.
  • Contributed to a wider gender-responsive capacity-development initiative promoting inclusion, academic leadership and institutional digital transformation.
National values education

Essential Values for Ghanaian Youth.

Vice Chairperson of Ghana's inaugural Youth Advisory Board and contributing writer to a nationally accessible values-education handbook developed for learners in second-cycle institutions.

Leadership · Youth Advisory Board

Vice Chairperson and national implementation support

As Vice Chairperson of Ghana's inaugural Youth Advisory Board, I have supported the development and implementation of the Essential Values for Ghanaian Youth initiative. My work has centred on helping ensure that the handbook reflects real youth language, school experiences and behavioural challenges rather than presenting values only as abstract concepts.

I also participated in the National Master Trainers Workshop at Ghana Senior High School in Tamale, where national facilitators were prepared to support school-level teams in implementing Values Learning Communities and peer-led values sessions.

Educational writing · National SHS resource

Contributing writer to the Essential Values handbook

Contributed to the writing and development process of the Essential Values for Ghanaian Youth Handbook, a practical resource structured around student-led learning, interactive activities, reflection and real-life scenarios.

The handbook contains 22 structured sessions covering 11 core values: responsible citizenship, honesty, integrity, diversity, equity, discipline, self-directed learning, adaptability, resourcefulness, leadership and building confidence. My contribution forms part of a wider effort to connect values education with responsible citizenship, social cohesion and the everyday decisions of Ghanaian youth.
