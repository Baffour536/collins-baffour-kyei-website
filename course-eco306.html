ECO 306

Project Appraisal II

An advanced and applied treatment of project appraisal that extends financial appraisal into economic and social evaluation. The course emphasises risk, economic efficiency, market failure, externalities, shadow pricing, distributional issues and Social Cost-Benefit Analysis in developing-economy contexts.

Learning focus

What students should be able to do.

  • Evaluate projects using economic efficiency and Social Cost-Benefit Analysis frameworks.
  • Quantify and interpret risk and uncertainty in project appraisal.
  • Apply valuation techniques to market and non-market goods.
  • Analyse equity and distributional implications of development projects.
  • Develop policy-relevant appraisal recommendations for Ghanaian and international development contexts.

Course roadmap

Topics and learning progression.

The roadmap below reflects the course outline.

Week 1

Introduction and Review of Project Appraisal I

Review NPV, IRR and BCR; transition to advanced appraisal; project appraisal and Ghana’s development strategy.

Week 2

Risk and Uncertainty in Project Appraisal

Project-specific, market and external risk; sensitivity and scenario analysis; energy and infrastructure applications.

Week 3

Economic Efficiency in Project Appraisal

Allocative, productive and dynamic efficiency; welfare economics; market failure and government intervention.

Week 4

Social Cost-Benefit Analysis: Foundations

Financial versus economic appraisal; measuring costs and benefits; discounting and the social discount rate.

Week 5

Valuation Methodologies in SCBA

Shadow pricing; opportunity cost; non-market valuation; revealed and stated preference; environmental valuation.

Week 6

Externalities and Social Optimality

Positive and negative externalities; private and social costs; taxes, subsidies and regulation.

Week 7

Distributional Issues and Equity in SCBA

Distributional weights; efficiency-equity trade-offs; poverty, regional disparities and rural electrification.

Week 8

Sectoral Applications of SCBA

Applications in energy, agriculture, transport, health and education; data limitations in developing countries.

Week 9

Advanced Topics and Course Review

Social discount rate determination; Harberger approach; integrated case analysis and final review.
