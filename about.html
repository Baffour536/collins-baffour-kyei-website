About

Academic profile

A quantitative economist with a growing interdisciplinary teaching and impact portfolio.

I am an economist, doctoral researcher and machine learning enthusiast at the University of Cape Coast, Ghana. My research interests sit primarily within applied econometrics, monetary economics, financial economics and machine-learning applications to economic questions.

My MPhil research examined the interconnectedness among commodity prices, banking-sector financial soundness indicators and selected macroeconomic variables. That work deepened my interest in economic relationships that vary across time, frequencies and market conditions, leading me to methods such as quantile regression, wavelets and TVP-VAR connectedness.

My PhD research extends that quantitative orientation to inflation-related issues in Ghana. I am particularly interested in empirical frameworks that reveal heterogeneity rather than relying only on average relationships.

Beyond research, I teach postgraduate data analysis and quantitative research methods, contribute to economics teaching texts, develop research software in R and support data-literacy and academic capacity-building programmes.

Research and teaching philosophy
How I work

Four principles that shape my research and teaching.

01 · Methodological fit

Begin with the question

I believe methods should be selected because they fit the theoretical question, data-generating process and identification problem—not because a technique is fashionable.

02 · Interpretability

Go beyond model output

Statistical and machine-learning results must be translated into defensible economic interpretations, with assumptions, limitations and uncertainty made explicit.

03 · Accessibility

Teach the “why” and the “how”

In quantitative teaching, I combine conceptual reasoning with practical software execution so students understand why a method is appropriate and how to implement it.

04 · Public value

Connect universities to society

Data literacy, research mentoring and community engagement are important pathways for translating university expertise into wider educational and development value.
