SOC 802

Quantitative Research Methods

An advanced introductory quantitative-methods course for postgraduate social science students, including learners whose prior methodological training has been predominantly qualitative. The course moves from research philosophy and design into measurement, sampling, EFA, inferential statistics, regression, logistic regression and introductory PLS-SEM.

Learning focus

What students should be able to do.

  • Understand philosophical and paradigmatic foundations of quantitative inquiry.
  • Operationalise constructs and assess reliability and validity.
  • Design sampling strategies and postgraduate-standard research instruments.
  • Select, execute and interpret appropriate statistical tests in SPSS.
  • Understand the conceptual basis and core outputs of PLS-SEM.
  • Report quantitative results accurately using APA 7 conventions.
  • Critically connect methods to research questions and theoretical frameworks.

Course roadmap

Topics and learning progression.

The roadmap below reflects the course outline.

Week 1

Research Paradigms and Quantitative Design

Ontology, epistemology, positivism and post-positivism; experimental, quasi-experimental, cross-sectional, correlational and survey designs.

Week 2

Measurement Theory and Variables

Operationalisation, scales of measurement, Cronbach’s alpha, content and construct validity, convergent and discriminant validity.

Week 3

Sampling, Questionnaire Design and EFA

Probability and non-probability sampling; Likert items; reverse coding; factor extraction, rotation, loadings and communalities.

Week 4

Foundational Statistics and Introduction to SPSS

Hypothesis testing, p-values, Type I and II errors, confidence intervals, effect sizes, descriptive statistics and normality.

Week 5

Inferential Statistics and Group Comparison Tests

t-tests, Pearson and Spearman correlation, chi-square, one-way and two-way ANOVA, ANCOVA and MANCOVA.

Week 6

Regression Approaches and Introduction to PLS-SEM

OLS assumptions and interpretation; binary logistic regression; reflective and formative constructs; live SmartPLS demonstration.

Week 7

Results Interpretation, Reporting and Design Consolidation

APA 7 reporting, practical versus statistical significance, SmartPLS interpretation and student-led methodological critique.
