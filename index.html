Current academic focus

Using richer empirical tools to answer economic questions that standard averages can miss.

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Cape Coast. My doctoral research focuses on machine-learning and advanced econometric applications to inflation-related questions in Ghana.

My wider research draws on quantile methods, wavelet analysis, time-varying parameter models, causality analysis and explainable machine learning. The common objective is to investigate how economic relationships change across states, horizons, frequencies and periods of stress.

Alongside research, I teach data analysis and quantitative methods to postgraduate students and support economics, econometrics, computing and policy-modelling courses.
